================================================================
  WIRING DIAGRAM — Bluetooth Hydroponics Pump Controller
  Arduino Uno + HC-05 + 5V Relay + DC Water Pump
================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------
  1. HC-05 BLUETOOTH MODULE
----------------------------------------------------------------

  HC-05 Pin    →   Arduino / Connection
  ---------        -------------------------------------------
  VCC          →   Arduino 5V
  GND          →   Arduino GND
  TXD          →   Arduino D2  (SoftwareSerial RX)
  RXD          →   Arduino D3  via voltage divider ⚠️

  ⚠️  VOLTAGE DIVIDER on HC-05 RXD (IMPORTANT):
  The HC-05 RXD pin is 3.3 V tolerant ONLY.
  Arduino D3 outputs 5 V — this will damage the HC-05 over time.

  Use a resistor divider to bring 5 V → ~3.3 V:

       Arduino D3 ──[ 1kΩ ]──┬── HC-05 RXD
                              │
                           [ 2kΩ ]
                              │
                             GND

  (A 10kΩ / 20kΩ pair also works — just maintain the 2:1 ratio)

----------------------------------------------------------------
  2. RELAY MODULE (controls the pump)
----------------------------------------------------------------

  Relay Pin    →   Arduino / Connection
  ---------        -------------------------------------------
  VCC          →   Arduino 5V
  GND          →   Arduino GND
  IN           →   Arduino D8

  Relay terminal wiring:
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  COM  ── Positive terminal of external     │
  │           power supply                     │
  │  NO   ── Positive wire of pump             │
  │  NC   ── Leave unconnected                 │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Pump negative wire → Negative terminal of external power supply.

  ⚠️  POWER WARNING — READ THIS:
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  NEVER power the pump directly from the Arduino 5V pin. │
  │  Even small pumps draw 200–500 mA on startup,           │
  │  which exceeds the Arduino's 200 mA total I/O budget    │
  │  and WILL damage the voltage regulator or reset the     │
  │  microcontroller.                                        │
  │                                                         │
  │  Always use a SEPARATE power supply for the pump,       │
  │  switched through the relay.                            │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

----------------------------------------------------------------
  3. COMPLETE CIRCUIT OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------------------------------

  [Phone]
     │  Bluetooth
     ▼
  [HC-05]
     │  TX → D2
     │  RX → D3 (via divider)
     ▼
  [Arduino Uno]
     │  D8 → Relay IN
     ▼
  [5V Relay Module]
     │  COM ← External PSU (+)
     │  NO  → Pump (+)
     ▼
  [DC Water Pump] ── Pump (−) → External PSU (−)

  All GNDs connected:
    Arduino GND = HC-05 GND = Relay GND = External PSU GND (common ground)

----------------------------------------------------------------
  4. RECOMMENDED EXTERNAL POWER SUPPLY
----------------------------------------------------------------

  Pump Voltage    Recommended Supply
  ────────────    ──────────────────────────────────────────
  3–5 V pump      4 × AA batteries (6 V) or USB power bank
  6–12 V pump     7.4 V 2S LiPo + LM2596 buck to pump voltage
                  OR wall adapter matching pump rated voltage

  Use a multimeter to verify pump voltage before connecting.
